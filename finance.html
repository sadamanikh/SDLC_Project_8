Estate Vistra Home Loan Buying Process

Buying a home is one of the biggest financial decisions you’ll make — and for most buyers in India, a home loan is an essential part of that journey. At EstateVistra, we’ve simplified the process into clear steps so you know what to expect, how to prepare, and what comes next.

step 1: Finalize Your Property First

Before applying for a home loan, first choose the property you want. This helps estimate the loan amount and compare offers easily.

step 5: Loan Eligibility Check & Assessment

The bank assesses your income, liabilities, credit score, LTV (75%–90%), and FOIR for loan eligibility.

step 2: Compare Loan Offers and Apply

Apply online or at a branch, negotiate the processing fee (0.25%–0.5%), compare fixed vs floating rates, and understand all charges before paying.

step 6: Receive and Review the Loan Offer Letter

After approval, the lender issues an offer letter with loan details, EMI, and terms. Read and negotiate if needed.

step 3: Submit Documentation

After choosing a lender, submit the application with documents like ID/address proof, bank statements, income proof, property papers, and photos.

step 7: Sign the Loan Agreement

After accepting the offer, sign the loan agreement. Under-construction homes may need a tripartite agreement, ECS/cheques, and insurance.

step 4: Bank’s Verification & Due Diligence

After application, the lender verifies your personal and employment details, credit score, property title/valuation, and conducts a field visit.

step 8: Loan Disbursement

After agreements and documentation, the bank disburses the loan—full payment for ready homes, or stage-wise for under-construction properties.

Some questions asked

What is an EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment)  is the fixed amount you pay every month to repay a loan.It includes principal + interest. Formula:EMI = [P × r × (1 + r)ⁿ] / [(1 + r)ⁿ − 1] Where:P = loan amount r = monthly interest rate n = number of monthly instalments

What is a Downpayment?

A down payment is the initial amount of money you pay in advance when you buy something using a loan. It is paid from your own savings.The bank or lender finances the remaining amount.

What is a Floating Rate of Interest?

A floating rate of interest is an interest rate that changes over time.It moves up or down based on market conditions.The rate is linked to a benchmark set by banks or the central bank.
